Who Runs What

Distributed ownership, shared infrastructure

The Model: We Build, You Own

This can't run on one person. It shouldn't. The whole point is that each stakeholder owns their piece. We provide the vision and the infrastructure. You bring your expertise, your relationships, your energy. Together we build something none of us could build alone.

Infrastructure & Vision

What We Do

  • Build & maintain the technology
  • Design the system architecture
  • Create pitch materials & documentation
  • Onboard new stakeholders
  • Aggregate anonymized analytics
  • Coordinate between domains (when asked)
  • Fix things when they break

What We Don't Do

  • Run the business discount program
  • Design the quests or storylines
  • Manage library integration
  • Organize community events
  • Make decisions for stakeholders
  • Control what data you share
  • Take a cut of transactions

Opt-In Analytics: You Control What You Share

The dashboard shows aggregate village data by default. But businesses can opt-in to share more-creating collective intelligence that helps everyone. You choose your level.

Level 1: Private

You see your own data. Nobody else sees anything about you.

"I just want the discount program. Keep my numbers to myself."

Level 2: Aggregate

Your numbers contribute to village totals, but aren't individually visible.

"I'm fine being part of 'total village visits' but don't show my specific numbers."

Level 3: Transparent

Other participating businesses can see your visit counts, trends, and patterns.

"I want to compare notes with the bakery and the bookshop. Let's learn together."

The Goal: A Self-Sustaining Ecosystem

When everyone owns their piece, the system doesn't depend on any one person. The library still runs book checkouts, the quest designer still writes storylines, and businesses still give discounts. That's resilience. That's community infrastructure.
