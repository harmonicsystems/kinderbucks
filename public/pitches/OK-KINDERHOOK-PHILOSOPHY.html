Five Types of Circulation

A living village has multiple circulatory systems. When any of them stagnate, the village suffers. VillageOS keeps them all flowing.

Money Circulation

Local dollars spent locally circulate 2-3x before leaving. Amazon purchases leave immediately. Kinderbucks and loyalty rewards keep money moving through Main Street instead of to Seattle.

Movement Circulation

Foot traffic is the lifeblood of a village. Scavenger hunts, trail badges, and check-ins turn passive residents into active explorers. People who move through space discover businesses, meet neighbors, and feel ownership.

Information Circulation

A village where nobody knows what's happening is a dying village. Shared analytics, event awareness, and collective memory keep everyone informed without requiring a Facebook addiction.

Social Circulation

Weak ties matter. The barista who knows your name. The neighbor you wave to. Badges and streaks create excuses for repeated interaction, turning strangers into acquaintances into community.

Engagement Circulation

Attention is finite. When it flows to Instagram, it's not flowing to your neighbor's art show. VillageOS creates local loops of engagement-quests, collections, achievements-that compete with global platforms for local attention.

Design Principles

1

Municipal, Not Corporate

This is infrastructure, like roads or streetlights. It should be owned by the community it serves, not by shareholders in another state.

2

Circulation, Not Extraction

Every feature should be evaluated by one question: does this keep things moving, or does this capture and hold?

3

Physical + Digital

Digital tools should drive physical presence. If it keeps people on their phones instead of in the village, it's failed.

4

Transparent by Default

Data belongs to the community. Aggregate analytics should be visible to everyone. Sunlight is the best disinfectant.

5

Opt-In Depth

Simple for those who want simple. Deep for those who want deep. A card works. Gamification is optional. Nobody left behind.

6

Federated, Not Centralized

Each village owns its instance. Cross-village features are opt-in. No single point of failure or control.
